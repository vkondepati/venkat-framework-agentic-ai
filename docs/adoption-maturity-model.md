# V.E.N.K.A.T Adoption Maturity Model

This maturity model helps organizations assess readiness for trusted Agentic AI architecture across the six V.E.N.K.A.T layers.

## Level 1: Foundation

The organization has basic data platforms, cloud infrastructure, dashboards, and isolated AI experiments.

Typical characteristics:

- Data quality and ownership are inconsistent.
- Events exist but are not treated as enterprise architecture primitives.
- Spatial and graph capabilities are project-specific.
- AI usage is mostly advisory or experimental.
- Governance focuses on access and compliance, not autonomous action.

## Level 2: Connected Context

The organization begins connecting trusted data, event signals, spatial context, and business relationships for selected domains.

Typical characteristics:

- Critical data products have owners, quality checks, and lineage.
- High-value business events are identified and instrumented.
- Location, routing, and proximity are used in operational use cases.
- Knowledge graph pilots connect entities and dependencies.
- AI workflows include human review and basic audit logging.

## Level 3: Governed Orchestration

The organization can coordinate agents, tools, workflows, and human checkpoints under policy controls.

Typical characteristics:

- Agents use approved tools through governed interfaces.
- Event-driven workflows trigger context enrichment and recommendations.
- Spatial and graph reasoning support operational decisions.
- Policy thresholds determine automation versus escalation.
- Audit trails capture decisions, actions, exceptions, and overrides.

## Level 4: Trusted Autonomy

The organization operates bounded autonomous systems for selected domains with measurable outcomes and continuous feedback.

Typical characteristics:

- AI actions execute within approved risk boundaries.
- Feedback loops improve data quality, reasoning, orchestration, and policies.
- Governance covers models, tools, data, workflows, humans, and outcomes.
- Architecture patterns are reusable across domains.
- Executive metrics link agentic systems to business value and risk reduction.

## Assessment Questions

- Which data products are trusted enough for AI action?
- Which business events should trigger AI reasoning or workflow execution?
- Where does spatial context materially change the decision?
- Which relationships and dependencies should be represented in a graph?
- Which tools can agents invoke, and under what policy constraints?
- What decisions require human approval, audit, rollback, or exception handling?
