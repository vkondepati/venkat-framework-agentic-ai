Applied architecture · Manufacturing

Warehouse and plant digital twin

Move the twin beyond visualization into governed recommendations, spatially aware workflows, and safe operational action.

Scenario

A manufacturer operates multiple plants and warehouses with production lines, inventory zones, automated equipment, maintenance crews, material paths, and strict safety constraints.

V.E.N.K.A.T response

V · Verified Data

Production, inventory, maintenance, quality, safety, and asset data have owners, lineage, freshness checks, and quality controls.

E · Event-Driven

Machine signals, work-order changes, inventory movement, quality exceptions, and safety alerts arrive in near real time.

N · Spatial Intelligence

The twin models plant layouts, equipment, worker zones, material paths, restricted areas, proximity, and travel distance.

K · Knowledge Graphs

Connect equipment, products, suppliers, maintenance history, process steps, operators, policies, and quality risks.

A · AI Orchestration

Agents recommend space optimization, maintenance priority, inventory movement, work orders, and supervisor escalation.

T · Trust & Governance

Safety-critical recommendations, shutdowns, and schedule changes follow explicit authority, approval, and audit policy.

Example outcomes

  • Improved space utilization and material flow.
  • Faster response to equipment and quality risk.
  • Predictive maintenance and governed work-order generation.
  • Worker-safety alerts informed by location and operating context.
  • Auditable recommendations, approvals, overrides, and actions.
