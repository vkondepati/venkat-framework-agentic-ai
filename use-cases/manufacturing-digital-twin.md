# Manufacturing Digital Twin Use Case

## Scenario

A manufacturer operates multiple plants and warehouses with production lines, inventory zones, automated equipment, maintenance crews, and safety constraints. Leaders want a digital twin that can move beyond visualization into governed recommendations and actions.

## V.E.N.K.A.T Response

### V - Verified Data

Production, inventory, maintenance, quality, safety, and asset data are validated and monitored for lineage, freshness, and ownership.

### E - Event-Driven Architecture

Machine events, work order changes, inventory movements, quality exceptions, and safety alerts stream into the architecture in near real time.

### N - Native Spatial Intelligence

The digital twin models plant layouts, equipment locations, worker zones, material paths, restricted areas, and travel distance.

### K - Knowledge Graphs

The graph connects equipment, products, suppliers, maintenance history, process steps, operators, safety policies, and quality risks.

### A - AI Orchestration

Agents recommend space optimization, maintenance prioritization, inventory movement, work order creation, and supervisor escalation.

### T - Trust & Governance

Governance controls ensure that safety-critical recommendations, maintenance shutdowns, and production schedule changes follow approval policies.

## Example Outcomes

- Improved space utilization.
- Faster response to equipment risk.
- Better material flow across warehouses and lines.
- Worker safety alerts with spatial awareness.
- Auditable work order automation.
